What is this dissertation about?

This dissertation situates itself in rhetorical computational argumentation — studying arguments through the audience's eyes. Motivated by growing political polarization, it asks: what makes an argument effective for one ideological group but not another? It addresses this across three tasks — quality assessment, style analysis, and argument generation — using news editorials, debate arguments, and large language models.
