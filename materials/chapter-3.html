Chapter 3

Argument Quality Through an Audience Lens

A computational framework for argument quality that captures how audiences with opposing political ideologies perceive an argument's effectiveness.

Assessment Corpus

Overview

Argument quality is rarely absolute — an argument that challenges one ideological group may reinforce another, or leave both unaffected. This chapter introduces a framework that defines quality through the lens of cross-profile effectiveness: how an argument lands differently for liberal versus conservative American audiences.

The framework distinguishes three audience responses — challenging (audience reconsiders an opposing stance), reinforcing (audience strengthens an existing stance), and ineffective (no change). A high-quality argument draws both ideological groups toward the same stance, narrowing the divide between them.

Key Findings

  • No more than 6% of editorials meet the proposed notion of high quality; only 1% changed any annotator's stance outright.
  • Cross-ideological persuasion is significantly harder than within-ideology persuasion — annotators sharing the same ideology show higher inter-annotator agreement.
  • The framework and corpus provide a reusable foundation for cross-ideological argument studies.
