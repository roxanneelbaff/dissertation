Chapter 2

Overview

This chapter situates the dissertation within argumentation theory, tracing the three classical disciplines — logic, dialectic, and rhetoric — and motivating the focus on rhetorical argumentation as the lens most sensitive to audience. It details the shift in computational argumentation (CA) from pre-LLM to LLM-era methods, providing important context for the methodological choices made across Chapters 3–5.

The chapter surveys related work across all three CA tasks addressed in the dissertation: argument quality assessment, style analysis, and argument generation. It highlights key gaps — particularly the absence of audience-centric modeling — that the dissertation's contributions directly address.

Tool Multi-Agent

Multi-Agent Pipeline for Related Work

Overview

To support the literature review for this dissertation, a multi-agent pipeline was developed to automate the exploration and synthesis of related work in computational argumentation. The pipeline retrieves, filters, clusters, and summarizes relevant papers — drastically reducing the manual effort required to map a rapidly evolving field.
